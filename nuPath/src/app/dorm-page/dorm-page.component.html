First Year Student Housing

I.M. Pei Residence Halls

Most first-year students are required to live in the Pei Residential Complex. These three buildings are often referred to as Pei 1st Court, 2nd Court, and 3rd Court.
The Pei residence halls accommodate more than 250 students in double and triple occupancy rooms, each with its own private bathroom. Community kitchen lounges are located in 2nd Court and 3rd Court. A shared laundry room is located in 2nd Court. The outdoor Palm Court around which the rooms are grouped is a focus of New College student life. Pei rooms measure approximately 15' x 15' and some feature covered porches or large balconies. The buildings' clustered construction, communal spaces and orientation around Palm Court afford residents a strong sense of community.

Pei 3rd Court is home to the Civitas Living Learning Community. This LLC is a First Year Community dedicated to helping new students transition into college life. They hold campus resource workshops, team & community building exercises, financial literacy workshops, and more.

Apartment-Style Residence Halls

Dort and Goldstein Residence Halls

The Dallas & Elizabeth Dort and Ann & Alfred Goldstein Residence Halls provide accommodations for approximately 150 students in apartment-style suites, each including its own living room, kitchenette and bathroom facilities. The Dort and Goldstein Residence Halls are located near the Recreation Center on the Pei Campus. The apartment bedrooms in this area are approximately 12' x 9' and are either three or four bedrooms.

Lodge-Style Residence Halls

The Letter Dorms: V, Peterson (W), Searing (X), and Y Residence Halls

The Lee & Bob Peterson Residence Hall (W), Ulla R. Searing Residence Hall (X), and the V and Y residence halls are lodge-style structures that feature studio, two- and three-bedroom apartments, each with its own kitchenette and bathroom. The halls feature a dramatic two-story floor plan with central common areas, timbered ceilings, and a keyless entry system. These side-by-side residence halls are sometimes called “Letter Dorms” because they were originally designated as V, W, X, and Y Dorms before two were officially named. The apartment bedroom sizes are approximately 12' x 9'.

The V Residence Hall is home to the Pride Living Learning Community. This LLC is dedicated to creating a safe and comfortable space for members to share in the exploration of identity, as well as learning about and celebrating LGBTQIA+ history and culture. Residents are placed without requirements of gender identity, gender expression, or sexual orientation.

The Peterson Residence Hall (W) is home to the Health & Wellness Living Learning Community. This LLC promotes hollistic wellness by hosting meditation groups, workout groups, stress-management workshops, and more.

The Searing Residence Hall (X) is home to the Global Village Living Learning Community. This LLC brings together students with a desire to learn more about international travel, culture, and the global community.

Finally, Y Residence Hall is home to the Performing Arts Living Learning Community. This LLC is dedicated to promoting students in their artistic endeavors. Join professional and student-run workshops, learn necessary networking skills, and show off you hard work at then end of semester showcase.

Pritzker Residence Hall (Z-dorm)

The Rhoda Pritzker Residence Hall, commonly referred to as Z Dorm because of its alphabetical designation in the original architectural plan, offers urban-style apartment accommodations. Each apartment includes its own eat-in kitchen, common area, and a suite bathroom. Common spaces include an open-air lounge and amphitheater, as well as the popular outdoor space known as Z-Green.
The Pritzker Residence Hall (Z) is home to the Writing, Sustainability, and Outdoor Adventure Living Learning Communities.

The Writing LLC is the perfect place for writers experimenting with all genres and mediums. Brainstorm in the in-house communal library, develop good writing habits, and more.

Sustainability LLC aims to help students live a sustainable life. Activities include contributing to a communal garden, foraging wild edible foods and herbs, participating in community service, and more. There are no prerequisites to apply for the Sustainability LLC besides a willingness to strive for a more sustainable lifestyle.

Outdoor Adventure LLC fosters a community for camping, hiking, spending time in the great outdoors, and preserving our natural resources for future generations.

Palmer B

Located on our historic Bayfront campus, Palmer B Dormitory, which is usually referenced as “B Dorm”, houses 32 students in single bedrooms with common lounges and bathrooms. Because of its somewhat secluded location on campus, B Dorm is popular among thesis students and is known for the community spirit that develops among its residents. Bedrooms come in two sizes: approximately 12' x 8' and 16' x 12'.
